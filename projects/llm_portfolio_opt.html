Goal

This project aims at finetuning the Chronos-T5 time series LLM with LoRA for portfolio optimization of ETFs using price based global factor data. This started in a monthlong hackathon where we tackled the problem: "How can LLMs be levraged to outperform the S&P 500?".

Process

Price based global factor data was generated from the adjusted HLOC values of our universe of ETFs. We then trained long only and long-short portfolio optimization models using a sliding window training and backtested using a sliding window scheme.

Highlights

We take the novel approach of using LLMs purely for time series analysis, not sentiment analysis and have a robust strategy which outperforms the S&P 500. When backtested over the period 2002 to 2024 the strategy yielded a sharpe ratio of 2.5.

Project Progression


The project originated when our team enterred the Society of Quantitative Analysts (SQA) alphathon, a months long hackathon where we tackled the question: "How can LLMs be leveraged to outperform the S&P 500?". We were selected as finalists for the competition and took the novel apporach of using LLMs strictly for time series analysis rather than text sentiment analysis.


In the competition we developed a portfolio optimization algorithm for the S&P 500, trained on time series technical indicators. During the competition we were given access to the QuantConnct algorithmic trading platform which we utilized for backtesting our algorithm.



After the completion of the hackathon we pivoted to performing portfolio optimization on a universe of ETFs (accross different industries) using price-based global factor data, outlined in the paper “Is There a Replication Crisis in Finance?” by Jensen, Kelly, and Pedersen. The equal weighted portfolio overall slighly underperforms the S&P 500, but our long only and long-short models outperform the S&P 500.
