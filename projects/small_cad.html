Brief Intro

The following are descriptions and reders of some small CAD projects that I have worked on while I was still a mechanical engineering major. Might as well display them here.


Reuleaux Machine

This is the "Inverse Slider-Crank Mechanism with Higher Pair Joints" within "The Reuleaux Kinematic Mechanisms Collection" at Cornell University. The device turns linear translational motion to circular motion. Originally designed by Franz Reauleaux and manufactured by Gustav Voigt Werkstatt.

Guillotine-Style Bagel Slicer

Kitchen tool designed to safely and evenly slice bagels in half. The guillotine style slicer has a safely shield with a slot to hold the bagel. The serrated blade is attached to the handle which moves vertically, slicing the bagel.

Open Design Project: Whetstone Guide Wedge (WhetWedge)

Keeps Knife at a constant angle whie sharpening it on a whetstone. The tool is adjustable for knives of different widths througgh the peg system and the angle can be adjusted by flipped the legs on the syste in out to account for the 2 most common sharpening angles for knives which are 20 (western-style) and 15 (Japaneese-style) degrees. This was 3D printed using PLA.
